About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Marine Geology and Geophysics at the University of Washington, advised by Dr. William Wilcock. My research focuses on microseismic activity at Axial Seamount, an active submarine volcano on the Juan de Fuca Ridge.

I develop machine learning and deep learning workflows — including transfer learning with PyTorch and TensorFlow — to automate the detection, classification, and source characterization of seismic signals. I am passionate about applying AI/ML technologies to geophysics challenges, from real-time signal detection to subsurface imaging and geohazard monitoring.

Research Experience

●

Ph.D. Research — Axial Seamount Seismology

University of Washington  |  2021 – Present

  • Designed, trained, and optimized transfer learning and deep learning models to automate classification of seismic signals and source mechanisms from waveform data.
  • Developed algorithms for processing continuous seismic data streams and extracting geophysical features to characterize subsurface structures of a submarine volcano system.
  • Conducted rigorous quality control across 10 years of research datasets to ensure accuracy and reliability of source mechanism results.
  • Presented research findings through peer-reviewed publications and oral/poster presentations at AGU and SSA.
●

Master Research — Oceanic Core Complexes

University of Chinese Academy of Sciences  |  2017 – 2020

  • Acquired, processed, and interpreted multichannel seismic and ocean bottom seismometer (OBS) data to characterize subsurface structures and oceanic core complexes.
  • Applied advanced seismic processing workflows: traveltime tomography, full waveform inversion, and reverse time migration to enhance subsurface imaging resolution.
  • Produced high-quality 3D seismic images of complex geological structures, resulting in a publication in a top-tier peer-reviewed journal.
